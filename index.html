Quantifying the Threat

Phishing

Phishing is popular because it works, it’s cheap to perform, and has little risk to the attacker of paying any consequences.

As a form of social engineering, the attack usually relies on a user performing some task, such as opening an email or clicking a link. Depending on the security configuration of the workstation, sometimes just viewing an email is enough to get compromised…[Read more]

Impact

The consequences of any user in your organisation falling for a phishing attack are always rising.

At first attacks impacted the user themselves, usually through some scam, or by being tricked into giving away personal data, but now the attacks are more ambitious in scope… [Read More]

Prevention

Preventing a successful attack is difficult, because it only takes one mistake for an attacker to succeed. However, it is not hopeless. Email security systems can react quickly to email marked as spam, protecting other users, but these require someone to first recognise the fake mail and take action.

This is where education becomes so important. Training employees to spot phishing attacks is more than just telling them to be aware.. [Read more]

Knowledge is Power, Go Deep.

1. How do I get caught?

You need to defend your whole enterprise. An attacker just needs to get lucky once. Your Anti-Phishing strategy must encompass multiple layers of prevention, identification, containment and quick remediation.

3. How do I identify a breach?

Average time to discover a serious breach is now many months. It can be hard, especially when an attacker is stealthy and uses encryption to hide their behaviour. There are no shortcuts, but some products and services are designed to do just this. Matta has a product called Malgorithmics which uses AI to hunt Malware.

2. What is the best prevention?

Education of users on a regular basis. Know what client software is running on your workstations and control and patch them regularly. If possible reduce the amount of client software. Do they really need Winzip, or that Quicktime Plugin?

4. Remediation Best Practice

Most companies will re-image the workstation rather than try to find out about the malware. If there are special circumstances, a forensic analysis can be initiated, but this must be done at the early stages.
