Impact of A Phishing Attack

Whatever figures you read or believe, the fact is that Phishing attacks are a significant cost to business each year. One of the more recent studies, which surveyed almost 400 IT professionals working at companies ranging from 100 employees to more than 75,000 in 2015, found that the 48% of the cost of a phishing attack was due to loss of employee productivity.

A further 27% of the cost of a Phishing attack was associated with loss of credentials which reportedly costs the average company in excess of £750,000 ($1m USD)

Since the Phishing attack is usually a means to implant malware, the cost of finding and cleaning up the malware was estimated by respondents to be £1.4m ($1.8m USD)

The total cost to the average company in the report was cited as £2.8m ($3.7m USD).

Numbers alone don’t tell the whole story though. The effort and loss of focus that happens after an attack – identified in cost terms as loss of productivity – translates on the ground to projects postponed, initiatives passed, and competitive advantages lost. If the company has to make a public statement, then if this is not handled well, it can result in executives losing the confidence of their stakeholders, and sometimes losing their jobs.
